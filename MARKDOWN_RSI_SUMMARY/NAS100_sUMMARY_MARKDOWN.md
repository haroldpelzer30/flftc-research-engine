# NAS100 H4 RSI Research Summary

## Research Objective

The goal of this research was to determine whether RSI-based mean reversion contains a measurable edge on NAS100 and whether additional filters improve predictive performance.

The following strategies were tested:

1. RSI Alone
2. RSI + Daily Structure
3. RSI + Double Bottom / Double Top
4. RSI + Inside Bar
5. RSI + Weekly Structure

All tests were conducted using:

* NAS100
* H4 Timeframe
* RSI Period: 14

# RSI Alone

## Results

| Setup | Best Win Rate |
|---------|-------------:|
| Oversold | 56.57% |
| Overbought | 43.89% |

Total Signals:

* 617

### Conclusion

RSI Alone produced a meaningful bullish edge on NAS100.

Bullish reversals improved steadily as holding periods increased.

Meanwhile, bearish reversals deteriorated significantly.

### Key Lesson

Overbought conditions on NAS100 frequently represented trend continuation rather than trend exhaustion.

# Major Findings

## Finding #1

### NAS100 Displays Strong Bullish Asymmetry

Across every test:

* Bullish reversals outperformed bearish reversals.
* Long-side opportunities consistently showed greater expectancy.

The market repeatedly favored buying weakness over shorting strength.

---

## Finding #2

### Overbought Does Not Mean Bearish

Across nearly every test:

* Bearish win rates remained weak.
* Average returns often remained positive after overbought signals.

This suggests that RSI overbought conditions frequently identify strong trends rather than exhaustion.

---

## Finding #3

### NAS100 Punishes Short Sellers

The bearish side consistently underperformed:

| Strategy | Best Bearish WR |
|-----------|---------------:|
| RSI Alone | 43.89% |
| Daily Structure | 49.02% |
| Double Top | 48.65% |
| Inside Bar | 41.33% |
| Weekly Structure | 55.00%* |

\* Extremely small sample size.

The data repeatedly suggests:

> Don't short NAS100 simply because it appears overbought.

---

## Finding #4

### Opportunity Count Matters

Higher win rates consistently came with dramatically fewer signals:

| Strategy | Bullish Signals |
|-----------|---------------:|
| RSI Alone | 175 |
| Daily Structure | 20 |
| Double Bottom | 32 |
| Inside Bar | 27 |
| Weekly Structure | 12 |

As filters became more selective:

* Win rates improved.
* Opportunity counts collapsed.

---

## Finding #5

### RSI Alone Is Surprisingly Difficult To Beat

Although several filters improved headline win rates:

* Most destroyed the majority of opportunities.
* Few provided enough additional edge to justify the reduction in trade frequency.

RSI Alone remained one of the strongest practical NAS100 strategies.

# Final Research Verdict

## NAS100 Summary

NAS100 demonstrated one of the strongest bullish personalities observed in the entire project.

The evidence suggests:

* Buying weakness contains a measurable edge.
* Shorting strength is consistently difficult.
* Trend persistence dominates bearish mean reversion.
* Additional confirmation often improves win rates but destroys scalability.

---

# Final Conclusion

The NAS100 chapter reinforces several major project themes:

> Long and short edges are often not symmetrical.

> A market can be so strongly biased in one direction that meaningful pullbacks become rare events.

> Don't over-filter a market that already wants to go up.

> A filter should be judged not only by how much it improves win rate, but also by how many opportunities it destroys.

> The market matters more than the indicator.

The evidence suggests NAS100 is one of the clearest examples in the project of a structurally bullish market where buying weakness is consistently more effective than attempting to short strength.
