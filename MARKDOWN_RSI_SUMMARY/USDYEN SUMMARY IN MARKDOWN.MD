# Major Findings

### Finding 1

USDJPY generated the largest RSI sample of any forex asset tested.

* 4,695 total signals

Yet produced the weakest RSI Alone performance.

---

### Finding 2

RSI Alone did not contain a meaningful edge.

This became the first major forex study showing that RSI extremes are not automatically predictive.

---

### Finding 3

Some filters improved performance modestly.

Unlike AUDUSD and NZDUSD, which generally showed little improvement from filters, USDJPY displayed small improvements when certain contextual filters were applied.

---

### Finding 4

The strongest improvements occurred on the bullish side.

Double Bottom and Inside Bar filters primarily improved oversold setups while bearish performance remained weak.

This supports the broader project finding that long and short edges are often not symmetrical.

---

### Finding 5

Results were highly dependent on holding period.

Many of the strongest statistics appeared only at specific horizons and failed to remain consistent across all evaluation periods.

---

# Research Interpretation

USDJPY behaved differently from every other forex asset studied so far.

### USDCHF

* Clear RSI edge.

### AUDUSD

* Small edge.
* Filters generally disappointing.

### NZDUSD

* Similar to AUDUSD.

### USDJPY

* No RSI edge.
* Some filters produced modest improvements.
* Improvements were inconsistent and horizon-dependent.

USDJPY became the most difficult forex asset to classify.

---

# USDJPY Conclusion

USDJPY provided some of the strongest evidence in the project that:

> An indicator is not an edge. An edge is an interaction between an indicator and a specific market.

RSI Alone produced approximately random results despite generating 4,695 signals.

Several filters improved performance modestly, particularly on the bullish side, but none produced a robust or consistently superior edge.

The USDJPY study reinforces multiple master-report themes:

* Opportunity count does not equal edge quality.
* RSI works on some assets and fails on others.
* Long and short edges are often not symmetrical.
* Consistency across horizons may be more important than a single impressive statistic.
* Different markets respond differently to the same signal.

USDJPY remains the most complex and least predictable forex asset studied so far.
