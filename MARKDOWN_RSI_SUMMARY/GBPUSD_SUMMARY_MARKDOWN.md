# GBPUSD H4 RSI Research Summary

## Executive Summary

GBPUSD emerged as one of the most statistically convincing assets in the entire forex study.

Unlike Gold, USDCAD, and major indices, GBPUSD did not display a strong bullish drift. Instead, it consistently exhibited a modest bearish mean-reversion tendency across multiple studies.

The most important characteristic of GBPUSD was not edge strength, but edge stability.

Across thousands of signals and multiple filters, the market repeatedly produced approximately the same result.

---

# Study Results

| Strategy                | Signals | Best Win Rate |
| ----------------------- | ------: | ------------: |
| RSI Alone               |   4,006 |        54.66% |
| RSI + Daily Structure   |   1,002 |        55.52% |
| RSI + Double Bottom/Top |   2,041 |        54.90% |
| RSI + Inside Bar        |     686 |        56.00% |

---

# Major Findings

## 1. The Edge Was Remarkably Stable

Across all completed studies:

```text
54.66%
55.52%
54.90%
56.00%
```

Despite large changes in filtering, the edge barely moved.

This suggests the underlying market behavior is more important than the specific confirmation method.

---

## 2. Bullish Reversals Were Mostly Random

Bullish performance remained close to 50% in every study.

Examples:

* RSI Alone: 50.86%
* Daily Structure: 49.00%
* Double Bottom: 50.95%
* Inside Bar: 48.51%

The long side showed little evidence of a meaningful edge.

---

## 3. Bearish Reversals Consistently Outperformed

Bearish performance repeatedly produced the strongest results.

Examples:

* RSI Alone: 54.66%
* Daily Structure: 55.52%
* Double Top: 54.90%
* Inside Bar: 56.00%

The edge was not large, but it was persistent.

---

## 4. Filters Reduced Opportunities More Than They Improved Results

Signal counts:

| Strategy          | Signals |
| ----------------- | ------: |
| RSI Alone         |   4,006 |
| Daily Structure   |   1,002 |
| Double Bottom/Top |   2,041 |
| Inside Bar        |     686 |

The filters removed large numbers of opportunities while producing only marginal changes in win rate.

This is one of the strongest examples in the project that:

> Added complexity frequently reduces scalability more than it improves edge quality.

---

## 5. GBPUSD Behaved Very Differently From Gold

Gold:

* Strong bullish asymmetry
* Structure mattered
* Double Bottoms mattered
* Location mattered

GBPUSD:

* Balanced market structure
* Weak bullish reversals
* Minimal response to Daily Structure
* Minimal response to Double Bottoms
* Minimal response to Inside Bars

Successful concepts from Gold did not transfer effectively to GBPUSD.

---

## 6. Market Behavior Matters More Than The Indicator

The same RSI framework produced completely different outcomes across assets:

| Asset  | Personality            |
| ------ | ---------------------- |
| Gold   | Bullish                |
| USDCAD | Strongly Bullish       |
| USDCHF | Balanced Positive Edge |
| USDJPY | Little or No Edge      |
| GBPUSD | Mild Bearish Edge      |

This reinforces one of the strongest conclusions of the project:

> An indicator is not an edge. An edge is an interaction between an indicator and a specific market.

---

# Final Assessment

GBPUSD is not the strongest asset studied.

However, it may be one of the most statistically reliable.

Strengths:

* Huge sample size
* Consistent results
* Frequent opportunities
* Stable behavior

Weaknesses:

* Modest edge
* Weak bullish side
* Filters provide little value

---

# Final Ranking of GBPUSD Strategies

🥇 RSI + Inside Bar — 56.00%

🥈 RSI + Daily Structure — 55.52%

🥉 RSI + Double Bottom / Top — 54.90%

4️⃣ RSI Alone — 54.66%

---

# Final Conclusion

GBPUSD appears to be a high-frequency, moderate-edge market.

The strongest lesson from the Pound is not that a specific filter works, but that most filters fail to materially improve performance.

The pair consistently produced a modest bearish mean-reversion edge regardless of the confirmation method used.

GBPUSD may ultimately become the best example in the project of a "workhorse" asset:

* Large opportunity count
* Stable behavior
* Modest but persistent edge
* High statistical confidence
