# EURUSD H4 RSI Research Summary

## Research Objective

The goal of this research was to determine whether RSI-based mean reversion contains a measurable edge on EURUSD and whether additional filters improve predictive performance.

The following strategies were tested:

1. RSI Alone
2. RSI + Daily Structure
3. RSI + Double Bottom / Double Top
4. RSI + Inside Bar
5. RSI + Weekly Structure

All tests were conducted using:

* EURUSD
* H4 Timeframe
* RSI Period: 14

---

# RSI Alone

## Results

| Setup | Best Win Rate |
|---------|-------------:|
| Oversold | 50.12% |
| Overbought | 50.84% |

Total Signals:

* 4,749

### Conclusion

RSI Alone produced results that were essentially random.

The enormous sample size provides strong confidence that EURUSD does not exhibit a meaningful RSI-only edge.

### Key Lesson

A large number of opportunities does not guarantee a market inefficiency.

EURUSD generated one of the largest datasets in the entire project while producing almost no predictive value.

# Major Findings

## Finding #1

### EURUSD Is Not Completely Random

Initial RSI Alone testing suggested EURUSD behaved like a coin flip.

However, multiple filters revealed a recurring bullish tendency.

The edge appears hidden beneath substantial market noise.

---

## Finding #2

### Bullish Asymmetry Emerged Repeatedly

Across every filtered test:

* Daily Structure
* Inside Bar
* Weekly Structure
* Double Bottom

the bullish side consistently outperformed the bearish side.

This suggests EURUSD possesses a mild bullish mean-reversion tendency when meaningful context is present.

---

## Finding #3

### Location Appears More Important Than Pattern Recognition

Results ranked:

1. Daily Structure
2. Inside Bar
3. Weekly Structure
4. Double Bottom / Top

The strongest result came from Daily Structure.

Pattern-based filters consistently underperformed location-based filters.

---

## Finding #4

### Daily Structure Outperformed Weekly Structure

Daily Structure:

* 56.25%
* 1,151 signals

Weekly Structure:

* 53.87%
* 761 signals

This supports the growing project hypothesis that:

> Daily Structure is close enough to influence H4 reversals, while Weekly Structure may be too distant and too coarse to provide meaningful predictive information.

---

## Finding #5

### More Confirmation Did Not Automatically Create Better Results

Several filters:

* Reduced signal count dramatically
* Improved performance only modestly

This reinforces a major project lesson:

> A filter should be judged not only by how much it improves win rate, but also by how many opportunities it destroys.

# Final Research Verdict

## EURUSD Summary

EURUSD initially appeared to be one of the most efficient and random markets tested.

However, deeper analysis revealed a different story.

The strongest evidence suggests:

* EURUSD contains a modest bullish mean-reversion tendency.
* Daily Structure provides the most useful context.
* Pattern recognition contributes little predictive value.
* Weekly Structure is less informative than Daily Structure.
* The edge is weaker than Gold, USDCAD, and USDCHF but stronger than RSI Alone initially suggested.

---

# Final Conclusion

The EURUSD chapter reinforces several of the strongest themes discovered throughout the project:

> Location appears to matter more than pattern recognition.

> Daily Structure is more informative than Weekly Structure for H4 reversals.

> A market can appear random until the correct context is applied.

> The asset matters more than the indicator.

EURUSD ultimately demonstrated that even highly efficient markets may contain exploitable inefficiencies when viewed through the proper lens.
