# USDCHF H4 RSI Research Summary

## Executive Summary

USDCHF emerged as one of the strongest and most statistically convincing forex markets in the entire research project.

Unlike GBPUSD, which produced a modest edge across thousands of signals, USDCHF generated stronger win rates while maintaining a substantial sample size.

The most important finding was that RSI Alone already contained a meaningful edge, making it surprisingly difficult for additional filters to improve performance.

Unlike many other assets, however, a small number of filters did provide modest improvements, particularly on the bearish side.

# Major Findings

## 1. USDCHF Is One of the Strongest RSI Markets Tested

Unlike USDJPY, where RSI produced little edge, USDCHF generated:

* 54.62% bullish reversals
* 57.00% bearish reversals

across more than 1,000 signals.

This reinforces a major project lesson:

> An indicator is not an edge. An edge is an interaction between an indicator and a specific market.

---

## 2. RSI Alone Contains Most of the Edge

The baseline strategy was already strong.

Many filters failed to improve upon it.

This supports another major conclusion:

> RSI Alone is surprisingly difficult to beat.

---

## 3. Daily and Weekly Structure Failed

Both structure filters:

* Reduced opportunities dramatically
* Failed to improve performance
* Often weakened the strongest existing edge

This became some of the strongest evidence supporting:

> Daily Structure is not consistently transformative.

and

> Weekly Structure may not be meaningful context for H4 RSI reversals.

---

## 4. Pattern Filters Were More Useful Than Location Filters

Double Tops and Inside Bars produced better outcomes than Daily or Weekly Structure.

This suggests:

* Market behavior
* Failed continuation
* Compression

may contain more useful information than simple proximity to previous highs and lows.

---

## 5. The Bearish Side Was Consistently Stronger

Across nearly every study:

```text
Bullish
↓

54-55%
```

```text
Bearish
↓

57-60%
```

This produced one of the clearest examples of:

> Long and short edges are often not symmetrical.

---

# Final Conclusion

USDCHF provided some of the strongest evidence in the entire project that market-specific behavior matters more than indicator complexity.

The market repeatedly demonstrated a durable RSI mean-reversion tendency on both sides, with the strongest results occurring when RSI was combined with select behavioral filters such as Double Tops and Inside Bars.

Unlike many other assets, USDCHF showed that carefully chosen filters can occasionally improve performance.

However, the broader lesson remains:

> Most of the edge was already present in RSI Alone.

USDCHF remains one of the strongest forex assets studied and one of the most statistically convincing examples of a repeatable market inefficiency discovered during the project.
