# Preliminary Conclusion

The Doji filter produced spectacular statistics but an extremely limited sample size.

Strengths:

* Highest bullish win rate in the NAS100 chapter.
* Strongest average returns.
* Edge improves over time.

Weaknesses:

* Only 13 bullish observations.
* Statistical confidence is low.
* Difficult to treat as a robust standalone edge.

---

# Research Takeaway

This study reinforces another recurring project lesson:

> Extremely selective filters can produce extraordinary statistics while simultaneously reducing confidence in the result.

The Doji filter generated outstanding performance but very few opportunities.

---

# Research Note

> The NAS100 BB + Doji study generated the strongest headline result of the entire NAS100 chapter, including an 84.62% bullish win rate over six candles and a 0.7725% average return. However, these results were based on only 13 bullish signals. While highly impressive, the sample size is too small to support strong statistical conclusions. The findings are best viewed as evidence supporting the NAS100 bullish drift thesis rather than proof of a standalone trading edge.
