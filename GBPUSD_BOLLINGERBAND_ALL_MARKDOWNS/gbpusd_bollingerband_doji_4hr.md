# Key Findings

## Observation 1

Doji produced the lowest signal count of any GBPUSD Bollinger Band filter tested.

| Strategy | Signals |
|---------|---------:|
| BB Alone | 4,675 |
| BB + Doji | 276 |

Opportunity Reduction:

**~94.1%**

---

## Observation 2

Unlike EURUSD, GBPUSD Doji did not completely fail.

Bearish Side:

* 50.38%
* 53.44%
* 56.49%

Performance actually improved as the holding period increased.

---

## Observation 3

A bearish asymmetry remains visible.

The strongest result occurred on:

```text
Bearish Doji
6 Candles
56.49%
```

This mirrors the broader GBPUSD tendency toward stronger bearish reversal behavior observed throughout the RSI chapter.

---

## Observation 4

Average returns support the bearish reversal interpretation.

Bearish returns remained negative across all horizons:

* -0.0149%
* -0.0106%
* -0.0625%

This is consistent with successful bearish reversal outcomes.

# Preliminary Conclusion

GBPUSD Doji behaved very differently from EURUSD Doji.

While EURUSD Doji was one of the weakest filters tested, GBPUSD Doji produced one of the strongest bearish results in the entire GBPUSD Bollinger Band chapter.

However:

* Signal count is extremely small.
* The result may be statistically fragile.
* Additional assets will be needed before drawing broad conclusions.

---

# Research Takeaway

The GBPUSD Doji study serves as another reminder that:

> The market matters more than the indicator.

The exact same Doji concept that failed on EURUSD produced one of the strongest bearish results on GBPUSD.

This suggests the behavior may be driven more by GBPUSD's underlying market characteristics than by the candlestick pattern itself.
