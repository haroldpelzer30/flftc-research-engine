# FLFTC MASTER RESEARCH REPORT
# RSI vs Bollinger Bands (H4 Study)

## Research Objective

Determine whether RSI or Bollinger Bands provide a more reliable framework for identifying market inefficiencies across:

- EURUSD
- GBPUSD
- USDCHF
- AUDUSD
- NZDUSD
- USDCAD
- USDJPY
- Gold (XAUUSD)
- SP500
- NAS100

All studies were conducted on the H4 timeframe using a consistent forward-testing methodology.

Evaluation Horizons:

- 1 Candle (4 Hours)
- 3 Candles (12 Hours)
- 6 Candles (24 Hours)

---

# Executive Summary

The original hypothesis was simple:

```text
Can Bollinger Bands outperform RSI
by identifying volatility extremes?
```

The answer was:

```text
Mostly No.
```

However, the final conclusion proved far more nuanced than expected.

Rather than discovering that Bollinger Bands were ineffective, the study revealed that:

> The effectiveness of Bollinger Bands depends heavily on the underlying market being studied.

# Major Discovery #1

## The Market Matters More Than The Indicator

This became the single most important finding of the entire project.

The same indicator often produced dramatically different outcomes depending on the market.

Examples:

### Forex

Bollinger Bands frequently generated:

```text
50%
52%
53%
```

results.

---

### SP500

Bollinger Bands generated:

```text
58.20%
519 signals
```

---

### NAS100

Bollinger Bands generated:

```text
60.97%
549 signals
```

The indicator did not change.

The market changed.

---

Across nearly every Forex market:

```text
EURUSD
GBPUSD
USDCHF

NZDUSD
USDCAD
USDJPY
```


# Major Discovery #3

## Bollinger Bands Struggled In Forex

This was one of the biggest surprises.

Initial expectation:

```text
Volatility Expansion

Opportunity
```

```text

```


results.

# Major Discovery #4

## Gold Needed Location

Gold produced some of the strongest Bollinger results.

However:

### BB Alone

```text
53%
```

### BB + Daily Structure

```text
60%
```

Huge improvement.

This reinforced an important lesson:

> Volatility identifies opportunities. Location determines whether those opportunities matter.

# Major Discovery #10

## Bullish Drift Dominated Index Results

SP500 and NAS100 repeatedly demonstrated:

```text
Oversold Volatility
+
Bullish Drift
=
Bullish Opportunity
```

Even bearish setups frequently failed.

Example:

### NAS100 BB Alone

Bearish Side:

```text
41.43%
45.00%
46.07%
```

The market simply preferred moving higher.

# Master Report Lessons

## Lesson 1

> The market matters more than the indicator.

---

## Lesson 2

> Don't fall in love with indicators. Fall in love with evidence.

---

## Lesson 3

> Volatility identifies opportunities. Location determines whether those opportunities matter.

---

## Lesson 4

> Strongly trending markets may already contain most of the edge before additional filtering is applied.

---

## Lesson 5

> An edge should be evaluated by both its strength and its frequency.

---

## Lesson 6

> A higher win rate is not automatically a better strategy if opportunity count collapses.

---

## Lesson 7

> Don't over-filter a market that already wants to go up.

---

# Final Verdict

RSI won the overall competition.

```text
RSI 8
BB 2
```

However, the project did not conclude that Bollinger Bands are ineffective.

Instead, the project discovered:

```text
RSI is the more universal tool.

Bollinger Bands are the more market-dependent tool.
```

RSI worked almost everywhere.

Bollinger Bands worked best when paired with markets exhibiting strong structural bullish drift.

---

# Final Research Conclusion

The most important outcome of the project was not identifying a perfect indicator.

The most important outcome was discovering that market behavior itself is the primary source of the edge.

Indicators merely help reveal it.

```text
The edge was never inside RSI.

The edge was never inside Bollinger Bands.

The edge was inside the market.
```

That realization became the defining lesson of the entire research project.
