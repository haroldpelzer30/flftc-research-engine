# Key Findings

## Observation 1

AUDUSD generated a very high number of Bollinger Band opportunities.

| Asset | Total Signals |
|---------|---------:|
| EURUSD | 5,274 |
| GBPUSD | 4,675 |
| AUDUSD | 4,663 |
| USDCHF | 1,248 |

AUDUSD looks much more like EURUSD and GBPUSD than USDCHF.

---

## Observation 2

The bullish side showed a very small edge.

Best Result:

```text
51.86%
```

over 3 candles.

However, the edge is extremely small.

---

## Observation 3

The bearish side failed completely.

Results deteriorated as the holding period increased:

```text
50.46%
49.07%
47.50%
```

The longer the holding period, the worse the performance became.

---

## Observation 4

Average returns remain extremely small.

Bullish Side:

```text
-0.0024%
-0.0099%
0.0104%
```

Bearish Side:

```text
-0.0068%
-0.0183%
-0.0109%
```

This is another example where Bollinger Bands generated many signals but very little economic value.

# Preliminary Conclusion

AUDUSD appears to be another example of:

```text
Lots of opportunities
↓
Very little edge
```

Unlike USDCHF, which generated fewer opportunities but stronger performance, AUDUSD produced thousands of signals while remaining close to random.

---

# Research Takeaway

The AUDUSD Bollinger Band Alone study provides additional support for one of the strongest emerging themes of the project:

> **Opportunity frequency alone is not a proxy for edge quality.**

AUDUSD generated nearly four times as many opportunities as USDCHF, yet produced materially weaker results.

This mirrors previous findings from both the RSI and Bollinger Band chapters and reinforces the idea that market behavior matters more than indicator choice.
