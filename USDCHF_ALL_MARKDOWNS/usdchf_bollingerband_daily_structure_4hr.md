| Study | Signals |

| BB Alone | 1,248 |
| BB + Daily Structure | 150 |

Opportunity Reduction:

**~88%**

---

## Observation 2

Unlike EURUSD and GBPUSD, Daily Structure did not materially improve performance.

### EURUSD

* BB Alone: ~50%
* BB + Daily Structure: 56.87%

### GBPUSD

* BB Alone: 52.90%
* BB + Daily Structure: 56.33%

### USDCHF

* BB Alone: 53.75%
* BB + Daily Structure: 53.62%

Essentially no improvement.

---

## Observation 3

The bullish side showed improving average returns over time.

Bullish Average Returns:

* -0.0317%
* 0.0221%
* 0.0738%

This suggests some follow-through despite only modest win rates.

---

## Observation 4

The bearish side produced positive average returns despite reversal win rates above 50%.

This suggests the bearish reversals are relatively weak and may not be economically meaningful.

# Preliminary Conclusion

USDCHF behaved very differently from EURUSD and GBPUSD.

Daily Structure failed to provide the dramatic improvement seen on the other forex pairs.

Instead:

* Signal count collapsed.
* Win rates barely changed.
* Edge quality remained largely unchanged.

---

# Research Takeaway

The USDCHF Daily Structure study challenges one of the strongest emerging themes from EURUSD and GBPUSD.

While Daily Structure dramatically improved Bollinger Band performance on those assets, the same filter provided almost no benefit on USDCHF.

This reinforces another major project lesson:

> **A filter that works on one market may provide little value on another market.**

The market continues to matter more than the indicator or filter itself.
