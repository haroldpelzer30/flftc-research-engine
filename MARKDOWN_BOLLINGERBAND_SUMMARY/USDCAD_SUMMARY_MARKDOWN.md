# Major Research Lessons

## Lesson 1

> USDCAD continues to behave differently from most major currency pairs.

### Evidence

Across both RSI and BB studies:

```text
Fewer opportunities
Stronger results
```

appeared repeatedly.

This behavior persisted regardless of the indicator used.

---

## Lesson 2

> The market matters more than the indicator.

USDCAD was one of the strongest RSI markets.

It also became one of the strongest BB markets.

The recurring behavior appears to be a characteristic of the market itself rather than the tool used to measure it.

---

## Lesson 3

> A filter can reduce opportunity count far more than it improves performance.

### Example

BB Alone:

```text
596 signals
54.95%
```

BB + Daily Structure:

```text
198 signals
55.86%
```

The filter removed nearly:

```text
400 opportunities
```

to gain:

```text
0.91%
```

at one horizon.

---

## Lesson 4

> Some strategies fail not because they are terrible, but because they lack a coherent behavioral story.

### Evidence

Double Bottom / Top:

```text
53.37%
45.19%
45.19%
```

and

```text
48.72%
53.85%
48.72%
```

The results oscillated between success and failure without demonstrating persistence, decay, or a consistent market tendency.

---

## Lesson 5

> Extremely selective filters can create statistically fragile studies.

### Evidence

Inside Bar:

```text
47 signals
```

Doji:

```text
27 signals
```

The sample sizes became so small that practical usefulness and statistical confidence were both severely limited.

# Chapter Verdict

The Loonie represented Team BB's best opportunity to score a meaningful win during the forex chapter.

Instead, it reinforced many of the same lessons observed throughout the project:

* Low opportunity counts.
* Moderate win rates.
* Weak scalability.
* Filters frequently reduced opportunities more than they improved performance.

USDCAD did produce the strongest BB currency results observed so far, but none approached the strength, consistency, or scalability of the RSI studies.

---

# Final Loonie Conclusion

> **USDCAD appears to generate fewer extreme conditions than most major currency pairs, but when those extremes occur they may contain more predictive information than similar extremes in higher-frequency markets. This behavior was observed across both RSI and Bollinger Band studies, suggesting the phenomenon is likely a characteristic of the market itself rather than the indicator used to measure it.**
