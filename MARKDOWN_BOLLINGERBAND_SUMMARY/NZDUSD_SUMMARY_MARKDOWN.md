# Major Research Lessons

## Lesson 1

> Highly correlated markets often produce highly similar research outcomes.

### Evidence

AUDUSD:

```text
4663 signals
51.86%
```

NZDUSD:

```text
4612 signals
51.94%
```

The similarity between the two markets was remarkable.

---

## Lesson 2

> A filter can reduce opportunity count dramatically without creating additional predictive power.

### Evidence

Daily Structure:

```text
4612
↓
518 signals
```

Performance deteriorated despite the reduction.

---

## Lesson 3

> One study is a clue. Repeated studies are evidence.

The Double Top result suggested a possible bearish edge.

The Doji study independently produced similar bearish results.

This increased confidence that a genuine tendency may exist.

---

## Lesson 4

> A statistically interesting edge is not automatically a practically useful edge.

### Evidence

Doji:

```text
55.07%
138 bearish signals
```

Approximately:

```text
1 signal per month
```

The edge may be real, but scalability remains questionable.

---

## Lesson 5

> The bearish NZDUSD BB signal may be real, but it appears to be small, selective, and infrequent.

Supporting Evidence:

Double Top:

```text
53.52%
54.46%
51.94%
```

Doji:

```text
55.07%
55.07%
53.62%
```

The effect was replicated across multiple BB filters, but opportunity frequency remained extremely low.

# Chapter Verdict

The NZDUSD chapter represents the strongest Bollinger Band chapter among the commodity currencies.

Unlike AUDUSD, the Kiwi produced two independent studies that suggested a modest bearish mean-reversion tendency.

However:

* The edge remained small.
* Opportunity counts were low.
* Scalability remains uncertain.
