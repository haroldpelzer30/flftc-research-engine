# Key Insight

This test produced one of the most surprising findings so far:

- Double bottoms did not create a meaningful bullish edge.
- Double tops did not materially improve bearish performance.
- Signal count remained extremely high.
- GBPUSD appears much less sensitive to structural retests than Gold.

The market appears to treat many double bottoms and double tops as normal price movement rather than meaningful reversal events.

---

# Research Conclusion

GBPUSD continues to behave very differently from Gold.

Current evidence suggests:

1. RSI alone provides only a modest edge.
2. Daily structure does not improve bullish performance.
3. Double bottoms do not significantly improve performance.
4. GBPUSD appears more balanced and less structurally reactive than Gold.
5. Further edge may require a different source of information such as:
   - session behavior
   - volatility expansion
   - liquidity sweeps
   - trend filters
   - macro directional bias

At this stage, GBPUSD is showing that market-specific behavior matters, and successful concepts from Gold do not automatically transfer to currency markets.
