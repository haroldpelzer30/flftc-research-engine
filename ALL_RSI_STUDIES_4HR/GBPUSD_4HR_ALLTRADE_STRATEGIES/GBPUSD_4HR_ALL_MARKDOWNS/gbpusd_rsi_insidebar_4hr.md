# Key Insight

This test produced another important result:

- Compression alone is not creating a bullish edge.
- Inside Bars did not materially improve long-side expectancy.
- The market continued to show stronger bearish tendencies than bullish tendencies.
- GBPUSD appears resistant to many of the structural concepts that worked well in Gold.

The data suggests that:

- Daily Structure = weak
- Double Bottoms = weak
- Inside Bars = weak

At least when combined with RSI alone.

---

# Research Conclusion

GBPUSD continues to demonstrate a very different market personality than Gold.

Current evidence suggests:

1. RSI alone provides only a modest edge.
2. Daily Structure does not improve bullish reversals.
3. Double Bottoms do not improve bullish reversals.
4. Inside Bars do not improve bullish reversals.
5. Bearish setups consistently outperform bullish setups.

The strongest recurring pattern discovered so far is:

- Slight bearish persistence
- Gradually improving short-side performance
- Weak responsiveness to structural retests

This suggests that GBPUSD may be driven more by:
- momentum
- session flows
- macro events
- liquidity behavior

than by traditional support/resistance retests.
