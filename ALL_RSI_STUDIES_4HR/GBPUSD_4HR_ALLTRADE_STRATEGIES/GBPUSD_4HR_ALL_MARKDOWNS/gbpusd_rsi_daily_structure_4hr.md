# Key Insight

One of the strongest findings from this test is that:

- Daily structure did not create a bullish edge.
- Daily structure slightly strengthened bearish reversals.
- GBPUSD appears much more balanced than Gold.
- Structure alone may not be sufficient to identify high-probability reversals in GBPUSD.

This suggests that GBPUSD may require additional behavioral confirmation, such as:

- Double bottoms
- Double tops
- Failed continuation patterns
- Liquidity grab behavior

before a meaningful edge emerges.

---

# Research Conclusion

The Daily Structure filter was not a major improvement for GBPUSD.

While bearish signals showed a modest increase in effectiveness, bullish signals remained near random and produced negative average returns.

Current evidence suggests:

1. RSI alone is weak on GBPUSD.
2. Daily structure alone is not enough.
3. GBPUSD may rely more heavily on failed continuation behavior than structural location.
4. The next critical test is RSI + Double Bottom / Double Top.

This test establishes that GBPUSD behaves very differently from Gold and may require a different framework to uncover a durable edge.
