# Observations

- GBPUSD generated a very large sample size.
- RSI alone produced only a modest edge.
- Bullish reversals showed almost no persistence beyond the first candle.
- Bearish reversals gradually strengthened over longer holding periods.
- Unlike Gold and SP500, GBPUSD displayed no obvious bullish asymmetry.
- Market behavior appeared more balanced between long and short opportunities.

# Key Insight

GBPUSD appears to behave differently than Gold and SP500.

Gold research suggested:
- bullish asymmetry
- structure-driven reversals
- persistence over time

SP500 research suggested:
- long-side advantage
- index drift
- trend persistence

GBPUSD currently suggests:

- weaker standalone RSI edge
- balanced directional behavior
- less natural long-side bias
- greater importance of additional filters

---

# Research Conclusion

RSI alone does not appear sufficient for GBPUSD swing trading.

The large sample size provides confidence that the result is meaningful:

- Oversold RSI alone is close to random.
- Overbought RSI alone shows a slight bearish edge.
- Additional context such as structure, double bottoms/tops, and higher timeframe confluence will likely be required to create a robust GBPUSD framework.

This establishes the baseline for future GBPUSD H4 testing.
