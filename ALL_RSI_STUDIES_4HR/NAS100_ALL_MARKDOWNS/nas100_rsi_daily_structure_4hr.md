# Key Findings

### Observation 1

Daily Structure dramatically reduced signal count.

* RSI Alone: 617 signals
* Daily Structure: 122 signals

### Observation 2

Bullish performance failed to improve.

* RSI Alone Bullish Best: 56.57%
* Daily Structure Bullish Best: 55.00%

### Observation 3

Bearish performance remained weak.

* RSI Alone Bearish Best: 43.89%
* Daily Structure Bearish Best: 49.02%

### Observation 4

Average returns remained strongly positive on the bearish side despite overbought conditions.

This suggests NAS100 continued trending upward even after extreme RSI readings.

### Observation 5

The bullish sample size was extremely small.

* Only 20 bullish signals

This significantly limits statistical confidence.

---

# Preliminary Conclusion

Daily Structure did not improve the RSI edge on NAS100.

Unlike EURUSD and Gold, Daily Structure appears to add little value to NAS100 mean-reversion signals.

The dominant characteristic remains:

* Strong bullish drift
* Weak bearish reversals
* Persistent upward trend behavior

---

# Key Insight

Daily Structure failed to improve NAS100 despite reducing opportunities by roughly 80%.

This reinforces a growing project lesson:

> A filter should be judged not only by how much it improves win rate, but also by how many opportunities it destroys.

In this case:

* Opportunity count collapsed.
* Win rate declined.
* The original RSI signal remained superior.

---

# Research Takeaway

NAS100 continues to behave similarly to SP500.

The data suggests:

* Buy-the-dip behavior remains dominant.
* Overbought conditions are poor short signals.
* Daily Structure provides little additional information.
* The underlying market drift appears more important than structural location.

Current evidence suggests that NAS100's edge comes primarily from its long-term bullish nature rather than proximity to previous daily highs or lows.
