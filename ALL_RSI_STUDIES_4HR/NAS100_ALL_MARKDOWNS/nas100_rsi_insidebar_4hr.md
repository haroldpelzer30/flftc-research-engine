# Key Findings

### Observation 1

Inside Bars produced extremely few bullish opportunities.

* Only 27 bullish signals
* One of the smallest bullish samples observed in the NAS100 research

### Observation 2

Bullish short-term performance was strong.

* 1 Candle: 62.96%
* 6 Candles: 55.56%

The strongest result occurred immediately after the signal.

### Observation 3

Bearish performance collapsed.

* 1 Candle: 41.33%
* 3 Candles: 40.00%
* 6 Candles: 32.00%

The bearish side performed worse than every previous NAS100 test.

### Observation 4

Average bearish returns strongly favored the bulls.

Even after overbought Inside Bar conditions:

* Price continued moving higher.
* Returns increased dramatically over longer holding periods.

### Observation 5

The strongest NAS100 bearish lesson continues to repeat:

> Overbought does not mean bearish.

---

# Preliminary Conclusion

Inside Bars appear capable of identifying high-quality bullish reversals on NAS100.

However:

* Sample size is extremely small.
* Trade frequency collapsed dramatically.
* Statistical confidence remains limited.

Meanwhile:

* Bearish Inside Bars performed extremely poorly.
* NAS100 continued punishing short-side mean-reversion attempts.

# Key Insight

Inside Bars produced the highest win rate observed so far on NAS100.

However:

* Only 27 bullish signals were generated.
* Signal count declined by over 84% compared to RSI Alone.

This reinforces another major project lesson:

> A filter should be judged not only by how much it improves win rate, but also by how many opportunities it destroys.

---

# Research Takeaway

NAS100 continues to display powerful bullish asymmetry.

The bullish side remains viable across multiple filters, while the bearish side consistently deteriorates.

Current evidence suggests:

* Buying weakness may contain a measurable edge.
* Shorting strength remains highly unfavorable.
* Trend persistence dominates bearish mean-reversion attempts.
* NAS100's long-term upward drift remains the defining characteristic of the dataset.
