# Key Observations

### Observation 1

NAS100 displays a clear bullish asymmetry.

Oversold reversals consistently outperformed overbought reversals across all holding periods.

### Observation 2

Bullish reversals strengthened over time.

* 1 Candle: 54.29%
* 3 Candles: 54.86%
* 6 Candles: 56.57%

The strongest result occurred at the 6-candle horizon.

### Observation 3

Bearish reversals deteriorated dramatically over time.

* 1 Candle: 43.89%
* 3 Candles: 41.40%
* 6 Candles: 39.59%

The longer a short position was held, the worse it performed.

### Observation 4

Average returns strongly support the bullish drift hypothesis.

Even after overbought signals:

* Average returns remained positive.
* Returns increased as holding periods increased.

This suggests NAS100 continued trending higher despite being overbought.

### Observation 5

Signal count remained relatively low.

* 617 total signals
* Approximately 62 signals per year over a 10-year sample

This indicates true RSI extremes occur less frequently on NAS100 than on major forex pairs.

# Key Insight

NAS100 appears to behave similarly to SP500.

The data suggests:

* Buying oversold conditions has merit.
* Shorting overbought conditions is extremely difficult.
* Long-term upward drift overwhelms many bearish reversal attempts.

This behavior is consistent with a growth-oriented stock index that has historically experienced persistent long-term appreciation.

---

# Preliminary Conclusion

RSI Alone on NAS100 produced a meaningful bullish mean-reversion edge.

The strongest result observed was:

* RSI Oversold
* 6-Candle Holding Period
* **56.57% Win Rate**

At the same time:

* Overbought reversals performed very poorly.
* NAS100 demonstrated strong resistance to bearish mean-reversion strategies.

---

# Research Verdict

**Status:** Promising

Current Evidence Suggests:

* Bullish mean reversion exists.
* Bearish mean reversion is weak.
* NAS100 shares many characteristics with SP500.
* Long-side opportunities appear significantly more attractive than short-side opportunities.

---

# Research Takeaway

NAS100 provides additional evidence for one of the strongest themes emerging from the project:

> Long and short edges are often not symmetrical.

The same RSI signal produced materially different outcomes depending on direction.

The bullish side demonstrated a measurable edge, while the bearish side consistently underperformed.

This supports the hypothesis that stock indices possess structural bullish drift that favors long-side mean reversion and weakens short-side reversal strategies.
