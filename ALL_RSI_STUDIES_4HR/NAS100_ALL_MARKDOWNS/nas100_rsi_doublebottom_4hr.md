# Key Findings

### Observation 1

Double Bottom signals were extremely rare.

* Only 32 bullish signals were identified.
* This represents a very small sample size.

### Observation 2

Bullish performance improved substantially.

* RSI Alone Bullish Best: 56.57%
* Double Bottom Bullish Best: 62.50%

### Observation 3

Bearish performance remained weak.

* RSI Alone Bearish Best: 43.89%
* Double Top Bearish Best: 48.65%

### Observation 4

Average bearish returns remained strongly positive.

Even after overbought double-top conditions:

* Price continued moving higher on average.
* Longer holding periods produced larger gains against short positions.

### Observation 5

The strongest result occurred on the bullish side:

* RSI Oversold
* Double Bottom Confirmation
* 3-Candle Holding Period
* **62.50% Win Rate**

---

# Preliminary Conclusion

Double Bottom confirmation appears to improve NAS100 bullish reversal quality.

However:

* Sample size is extremely small.
* Statistical confidence remains limited.
* Additional data would be needed before considering the result robust.

Meanwhile:

* Double Tops failed to create a meaningful bearish edge.
* NAS100 continued displaying strong resistance to short-side mean reversion.

# Key Insight

Double Bottom confirmation may help identify higher-quality bullish reversals on NAS100.

However, the signal count collapsed dramatically:

* RSI Alone: 617 signals
* Double Bottom: 217 signals
* Bullish Double Bottoms: only 32 signals

This reinforces another major project lesson:

> A small increase in win rate is not automatically an improvement if it comes at the cost of most of your opportunities.

---

# Research Takeaway

NAS100 continues to exhibit strong bullish asymmetry.

The bullish side improved further when Double Bottom confirmation was added, while the bearish side remained ineffective.

Current evidence suggests:

* Buying weakness may contain an edge.
* Shorting strength remains difficult.
* Double Bottoms may help identify higher-quality long setups.
* The long-term upward drift of NAS100 continues to dominate the data.
