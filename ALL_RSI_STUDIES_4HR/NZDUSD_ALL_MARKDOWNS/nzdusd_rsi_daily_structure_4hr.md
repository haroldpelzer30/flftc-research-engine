# Key Findings

### 1. Significant Reduction in Opportunities

Signal count fell from:

* 3,343 RSI Alone signals

to:

* 640 Daily Structure signals

representing an approximately 81% reduction in opportunities.

---

### 2. Minimal Improvement

Best result achieved:

* Bearish
* 1 Candle Hold
* 53.31%

This represents only a marginal improvement over the 52.86% achieved by RSI Alone.

---

### 3. Long-Term Performance Remained Weak

The 6-candle results were disappointing:

* Bullish: 52.92%
* Bearish: 46.08%

The bearish side deteriorated substantially over longer holding periods.

---

# Research Interpretation

Unlike AUDUSD, where Daily Structure produced a noticeable bullish improvement, NZDUSD showed very little benefit from the Daily Structure filter.

The filter reduced opportunity count dramatically while producing only a marginal increase in win rate.

This result continues a growing theme throughout the project:

> Filters often reduce opportunities far more than they improve edge quality.

---

# Preliminary Conclusion

NZDUSD RSI + Daily Structure failed to demonstrate a meaningful advantage over RSI Alone.

Current NZDUSD ranking:

1. RSI + Daily Structure — 53.31%
2. RSI Alone — 52.86%

The difference is small relative to the substantial reduction in trade opportunities.

At this stage, Daily Structure appears far less impactful on NZDUSD than it was on AUDUSD.
