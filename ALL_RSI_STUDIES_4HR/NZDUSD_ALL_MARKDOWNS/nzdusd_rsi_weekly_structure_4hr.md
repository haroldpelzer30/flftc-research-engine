# Key Findings

### 1. Weekly Structure Failed on the Bullish Side

Bullish performance was extremely weak:

| Horizon   | Win Rate |
| --------- | -------- |
| 1 Candle  | 46.23%   |
| 3 Candles | 46.73%   |
| 6 Candles | 40.70%   |

The 6-candle result was one of the weakest outcomes observed in the entire NZDUSD study.

---

### 2. Bearish Side Showed Limited Strength

The strongest result was:

* Bearish
* 1-Candle Hold
* 54.39%

However, the edge deteriorated quickly beyond the first candle.

---

### 3. Opportunity Count Collapsed

Signal count fell from:

* 3,343 RSI Alone signals

to:

* 427 Weekly Structure signals

representing an approximately 87% reduction in opportunities.

---

# Research Interpretation

Weekly Structure once again failed to provide meaningful predictive value for H4 RSI reversals.

The filter dramatically reduced opportunity count while producing:

* Weak bullish performance
* Mixed bearish performance
* No consistent improvement over RSI Alone

This result closely mirrors the AUDUSD Weekly Structure study.

# Preliminary Conclusion

NZDUSD RSI + Weekly Structure does not provide compelling evidence that weekly highs and lows improve H4 RSI mean-reversion signals.

The study provides additional support for the project's growing hypothesis:

> Weekly Structure may be too distant and too coarse to provide meaningful context for H4 reversals occurring over a 12–24 hour horizon.
