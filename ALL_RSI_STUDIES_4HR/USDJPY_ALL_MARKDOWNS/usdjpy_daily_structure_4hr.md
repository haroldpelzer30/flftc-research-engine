# Key Findings

### 1. Daily Structure Made USDJPY Worse

RSI Alone produced:

* 50.29% best result

RSI + Daily Structure produced:

* 47.54% best result

The filter reduced performance rather than improving it.

---

### 2. Massive Reduction in Opportunities

Signal count fell from:

* 4,695 RSI Alone signals

to:

* 1,022 Daily Structure signals

representing a reduction of approximately 78%.

---

### 3. Both Bullish and Bearish Sides Failed

Bullish:

* 46.96%
* 47.54%
* 47.54%

Bearish:

* 46.38%
* 46.23%
* 46.68%

Neither side demonstrated a meaningful edge.

---

### 4. Average Returns Were Consistent with Weak Performance

Bullish average returns became increasingly negative as holding periods increased.

This suggests oversold conditions frequently occurred during sustained downward movement rather than reversal zones.

---

# Research Interpretation

This is one of the strongest examples in the entire project of a filter making a strategy worse.

The Daily Structure filter:

* Reduced opportunities dramatically.
* Failed to improve win rate.
* Reduced win rate below the already weak RSI Alone baseline.

This directly contradicts the common assumption that additional confirmation automatically improves trade quality.

---

# Preliminary Conclusion

USDJPY RSI + Daily Structure produced one of the weakest results observed in the forex study.

Current USDJPY ranking:

1. RSI Alone — 50.29%
2. RSI + Daily Structure — 47.54%

The study provides further evidence that:

* Additional filters do not guarantee better performance.
* Daily Structure is not universally useful.
* The effectiveness of a filter depends heavily on the underlying market.

USDJPY continues to support one of the project's most important conclusions:

> An indicator is not an edge. An edge is an interaction between an indicator and a specific market.
