# Research Interpretation

USDCAD continues to support the idea that:

> Long and short edges are often not symmetrical.

The bullish side exhibited one of the strongest reversal tendencies observed in the forex study, while the bearish side showed no evidence of a reliable edge.

The results also reinforce another emerging project lesson:

> A filter can improve a statistic without creating an edge.

The edge already existed in the RSI Alone study.

Daily Structure merely isolated a smaller subset of trades while producing only a marginal improvement in win rate.

---

# Preliminary Conclusion

Current USDCAD Ranking:

1. RSI + Daily Structure — 64.71%
2. RSI Alone — 63.87%

Although Daily Structure produced the highest headline win rate, the improvement over RSI Alone was minimal relative to the reduction in opportunities.

The study strengthens several master-report themes:

* Opportunity count does not equal edge quality.
* Daily Structure is not consistently transformative.
* A filter can improve a statistic without creating an edge.
* Long and short edges are often not symmetrical.
* Selective opportunities may contain stronger market inefficiencies.
