# Key Findings

### 1. Large Reduction in Opportunities

Signal count dropped from:

* 3,487 RSI Alone signals

to:

* 1,535 Double Bottom / Top signals

This represents a substantial reduction in trade opportunities.

---

### 2. No Meaningful Improvement in Win Rate

Best result achieved:

* 51.66%
* Bullish 3-Candle Hold

This failed to exceed the performance of RSI Alone and was materially weaker than the Daily Structure study.

---

### 3. Six-Candle Performance Deteriorated

Bullish results declined to:

* 49.09%

at the 6-candle holding period.

This suggests that identifying a second touch of a recent low did not improve AUDUSD mean-reversion performance.

---

# Research Interpretation

The Double Bottom / Double Top filter did not enhance AUDUSD RSI signals.

Despite cutting signal count by more than half, win rates remained near random expectation and, in some cases, deteriorated below the RSI Alone baseline.

This result provides further support for one of the emerging themes of the project:

> Most filters reduce opportunity count far more than they improve edge quality.

---

# Preliminary Conclusion

AUDUSD H4 RSI + Double Bottom / Double Top does not appear to provide a meaningful edge enhancement over RSI Alone.

Current AUDUSD ranking:

1. RSI + Daily Structure (Bullish) — 55.02%
2. RSI Alone — 52.10%
3. RSI + Double Bottom / Top — 51.66%

At this stage, the Double Bottom / Top filter appears to function primarily as a trade-reduction mechanism rather than an edge-enhancement mechanism.
