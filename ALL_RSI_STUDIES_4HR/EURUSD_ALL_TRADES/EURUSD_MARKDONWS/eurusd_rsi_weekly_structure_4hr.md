# Key Findings

### Observation 1

* RSI Alone: 4,749 signals
* Weekly Structure: 761 signals

Reduction of approximately 84%.

### Observation 2

Bullish performance improved relative to RSI Alone:

* RSI Alone Bullish Best: 50.12%
* Weekly Structure Bullish Best: 53.87%

### Observation 3

Bearish performance deteriorated substantially:

* RSI Alone Bearish Best: 50.84%
* Weekly Structure Bearish Best: 50.28%
* 6-Candle Bearish Result: 44.72%

### Observation 4

Weekly Structure failed to outperform Daily Structure:

* Daily Structure Best Result: 56.25%
* Weekly Structure Best Result: 53.87%

### Observation 5

Weekly Structure appears to weaken bearish reversals while only modestly improving bullish reversals.

---

# Preliminary Conclusion

Weekly Structure provided some improvement on the bullish side but failed to generate a strong edge.

Compared to Daily Structure:

* Lower win rates
* Fewer opportunities
* Weaker overall performance

This suggests that previous weekly highs and lows may be too distant to provide meaningful context for H4 mean-reversion signals.

# Key Insight

Daily Structure remains the strongest EURUSD filter by a meaningful margin.

Weekly Structure improved performance slightly relative to RSI Alone but failed to match the gains achieved by Daily Structure.

The evidence suggests that recent market location (daily levels) contains more useful information for H4 reversals than broader weekly levels.

---

# Research Takeaway

EURUSD provides additional support for one of the strongest emerging hypotheses in the project:

> Daily Structure is close enough to influence H4 reversals, while Weekly Structure may be too distant and too coarse to provide meaningful predictive information.

Current evidence indicates that location matters, but proximity matters as well. Daily levels appear significantly more informative than weekly levels for EURUSD mean-reversion behavior.
