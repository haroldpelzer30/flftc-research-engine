# Key Findings

### Observation 1

Double Bottom / Double Top preserved a large sample size:

* 2,141 total signals
* Nearly half the signal count of RSI Alone

### Observation 2

Bullish performance improved only marginally:

* RSI Alone Best Bullish Result: 50.12%
* Double Bottom Best Bullish Result: 52.95%

### Observation 3

Bearish performance remained near random:

* RSI Alone Best Bearish Result: 50.84%
* Double Top Best Bearish Result: 52.03%

### Observation 4

Daily Structure significantly outperformed Double Bottom / Double Top:

* Daily Structure Best Result: 56.25%
* Double Bottom Best Result: 52.95%

### Observation 5

Pattern recognition increased selectivity but failed to create a strong edge.

---

# Preliminary Conclusion

Double Bottom / Double Top does not appear to be a major source of predictive information on EURUSD.

While the pattern produced a slight improvement over RSI Alone, the gains were modest and materially weaker than those produced by Daily Structure.

This suggests:

* EURUSD may respond more strongly to location than to pattern recognition.
* Double Bottoms and Double Tops may be descriptive rather than predictive.
* Structural context appears more important than retest patterns on EURUSD.

# Key Insight

**Location appears to matter more than pattern recognition on EURUSD.**

This test generated over 2,100 signals yet failed to produce a meaningful edge, while Daily Structure generated fewer signals and materially stronger performance.

The evidence currently suggests that previous Daily Highs and Lows contain more useful information than Double Bottom and Double Top formations.

---

# Research Takeaway

This result reinforces one of the strongest emerging themes of the project:

> A filter should not be judged solely by how much it improves win rate, but also by how many opportunities it destroys.

EURUSD Daily Structure reduced signals dramatically but produced a materially stronger edge.

EURUSD Double Bottom / Double Top preserved a much larger sample size but failed to generate a comparable improvement.

Current evidence suggests that, on EURUSD, location-based context contains more predictive information than pattern recognition.
