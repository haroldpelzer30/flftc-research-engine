# Gold H4 Strategy Leaderboard

## Research Context

The following strategies were developed through quantitative research on Gold (XAUUSD) using:

- H4 execution timeframe
- RSI exhaustion models
- structure-based analysis
- failed continuation behavior
- statistical forward return testing

The research consistently showed that:
- structure matters more than indicators alone
- failed continuation behavior is highly informative
- Gold exhibits strong bullish asymmetry
- higher timeframe context improves reversal quality

# Major Research Conclusions

## 1. Structure Matters More Than Indicators

The strongest strategies consistently involved:
- Daily structure
- liquidity interaction
- macro location context

Indicator stacking alone produced weaker results.

---

## 2. Failed Continuation Is Powerful

Double bottoms repeatedly outperformed:
- inside bars
- compression logic
- generic indicator combinations

This suggests failed moves contain meaningful behavioral information.

---

## 3. Gold Displays Bullish Asymmetry

Across nearly all tests:
- bullish reversals outperformed bearish reversals
- bullish continuation persisted longer
- bearish edges deteriorated over time

This supports the idea that Gold possesses long-term upward drift characteristics similar to equity markets.

---

## 4. Higher Timeframe Context Improves Edge

The strongest results consistently involved:
- Daily structure
- H4 execution
- macro reversal context

This suggests timeframe synchronization is highly important for Gold swing trading.

---

# Overall Research Insight

The research increasingly supports the following framework:

1. Structure defines opportunity
2. RSI confirms exhaustion
3. Failed continuation confirms reversal intent
4. Patience and selectivity outperform excessive trade frequency

The strongest Gold strategies were:
- behaviorally coherent
- statistically respectable
- structurally aligned
- and persistent over longer holding periods.
